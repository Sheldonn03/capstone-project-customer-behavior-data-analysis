Behavior explains more LTV variation than demographics

Total purchases, session depth, pages per session, login frequency, email open rate, and wishlist items carry the strongest directional value signals.

Cart abandonment is the clearest friction flag

Abandonment is negatively associated with LTV, so checkout diagnostics should focus on high-intent users who still abandon carts.

Sentiment adds customer-experience context

Negative reviews are large enough to mine and should be paired with service, returns, and abandonment metrics before operational action.

Sources and methodology

Visible source notes keep the dashboard auditable without turning the page into a notebook dump.

Sources used: Report_ecommerce_customer behavior analysis_Sheldon.pdf, Data Analysis and Visualization/ecommerce_customer behavior analysis_Sheldon.ipynb, and Dataset/ecommerce_customer_dataset_with_reviews.csv. The charts shown here are the PNG outputs generated from the Python analysis workflow.

Cleaning basis: high-missing or out-of-scope fields were removed for EDA; numeric missing values were median-imputed; duplicate rows, invalid values, and outliers were reviewed before interpreting the charts.

Sentiment method: review text was scored with VADER compound sentiment. Scores at or above 0.05 were labeled Positive, scores at or below -0.05 were labeled Negative, and the middle band was labeled Neutral.

Dashboard caveat: this is a static dashboard generated from local files. It does not auto-refresh and should be regenerated after the notebook or source CSV changes.
